How to Use AeroSwarm

4 steps to trigger an emergency dispatch
1
Upload Surveillance Media
Go to Incidents and drag-and-drop any image or video — dashcam footage, drone camera, CCTV screenshot. Supported formats: JPG, PNG, MP4, MOV, WEBM.
2
AI Auto-Detects the Incident
The Watcher agent (Gemma 4 on Cerebras) analyzes the image using multimodal AI. It detects: drowning, vehicle accidents, fires, flooding, and casualties — no manual selection needed.
3
Nearest Appropriate Unit Dispatched
The system automatically determines which rescue type is needed and dispatches the nearest eligible unit. Water rescue → MARINE or HÉLICO. Road accident → BSPP, SAMU, or POLICE. Each gets a full incident brief before arrival.
4
Track on Paris Map
The Paris map shows all 8 units in real time. When dispatched, the unit marker turns gold and a route line appears. The tracking panel shows Detection → Analysis → Dispatch timeline with timestamps.
For Videos
Load the video → scrub to the moment of the accident → click 📸 Capture Frame → AI automatically analyzes the frame and dispatches the right rescue team. No manual scene selection required.

About AeroSwarm AI · FAQ
About AeroSwarm AI
AeroSwarm is a multi-agent AI emergency response system built for the Cerebras × Google DeepMind Gemma 4 Hackathon. It uses a 4-agent pipeline — Watcher, Analyst, Reporter, Dispatch — powered by Gemma 4 31B running on Cerebras inference hardware. Images are analyzed in seconds, the right rescue team is identified, and a detailed incident brief is transmitted before the unit arrives.
What incident types can AeroSwarm detect?
Person in water (drowning / capsized vessel), vehicle accident (collision, pileup, pedestrian struck), fire or smoke, flooding, and mass casualty events.
How does it pick the right rescue team?
The Watcher AI classifies the incident kind. A deterministic dispatch engine then filters the 8 Paris units to only those capable of responding (e.g. water rescue for drowning), and sends the nearest one using Haversine distance calculation.
What are the 8 active Paris units?
HÉLICO-1 (air rescue, Issy), MARINE-7 (water rescue, Bercy), MARINE-3 (water rescue, Levallois), SAMU-75 (ambulance, Hôtel-Dieu), SAMU-14 (ambulance, Lariboisière), BSPP-8 (fire brigade, Madeleine), POLICE-17 (police, Batignolles), POLICE-16 (police, Boulogne).
What is the Speed Race panel?
It runs the same image simultaneously through Cerebras (Gemma 4) and Google Gemini (GPU baseline) to compare inference speed. Cerebras typically responds 3–10× faster thanks to its custom silicon architecture.
